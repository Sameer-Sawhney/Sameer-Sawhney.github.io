Journey from "I.D.E.A" to MBA !

Life After Innovate Delhi

Posted by Sameer Sawhney on June 07, 2016

After winning I.D.E.A. (Innovate Delhi Entrepreneurship Academy) organized by Stanford GSB it was quite apparent that I wanted to learn more about Startups and contribute to the startup ecosystem in and around Delhi.

Little had I known when I started, that an entrepreneurial program would eventually prove to become my moment of epiphany. Revolutionary ideas, motivating speeches, world-class speakers - it was an incredibly vibrant atmosphere, and in many ways proved to be a stimulus that led me in search of a new sphere of working where I could independently bring tangibility to new ideas, and let the entrepreneur in me shine. The program was about how to innovate and find innovative solutions for challenging problems with technology and build products for the future.

I returned home to find myself instilled with refreshing confidence that I could be a successful entrepreneur one day. During the program, I learnt about the end-to-end process of venture design from Stanford professors and entrepreneurs from Silicon Valley. Their experience on topics such as the venture ecosystem, identifying market opportunities, design thinking, MVP, scaling a startup, and leading a venture was quite enriching. In the third week of program, we were divided in small teams to create a complete venture plan consisting of a market analysis, a mock up of the product, and a pitch to potential investors. My team presented the idea of ‘Surfing Couch’, a technology product to make global WiFi sharing possible. For 3 weeks, 24 hours a day my ideas were challenged, and it felt exciting to be in the shoes of an entrepreneur.

I wanted to challenge myself post I.D.E.A so I decided to pursue my MBA to gain the necessary business skills. With this new goal in mind, my MBA journey got kickstarted. I am quite excited that I will officially start my journey soon in Fall'16 at Olin Business School at Washington University, St. Louis

I hope to utilize the next two years to sharpen my business knowledge and continue to experiment with my ideas and hopefully bring to life some of them ! I am quite excited to start this new phase of my life.
