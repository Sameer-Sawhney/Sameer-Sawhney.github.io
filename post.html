Journey from "I.D.E.A" to MBA !

Life After Innovate Delhi

Posted by Sameer Sawhney on June 07, 2016

After winning I.D.E.A'14 (Innovate Delhi Entrepreneurship Academy) for "SurfingCouch" - A global wifi lending system, it was quite apparent that I wanted to learn more about Startups and contribute to the startup ecosystem around Delhi.

I.D.E.A provided me a platform to explore the startup circle in and around Delhi. I visited quite a few startups in coming months and learnt about how different startups survive, grow and sometimes shut down in the tough times

Learn by doing

I wanted to challenge myself post I.D.E.A. With my decade long experience of working on some of the leading world class software products, I wanted to check if I can build a complete software platform from scratch.

Working full time as a Software manager, I took up the challenge and with help of IITD resources, I was able to assemble a team of young students who were eager to refine their software skills

After 10 months of working on weekends, the team was able to build first version of a Social Media Analytics platform using open source technologies which got deployed at various organizations.

This experience gave me enough confidence that with right team, building a world class software is a matter of time if efforts are put in right place.

As the software platform we built was for non-commerical use, it was critical that I also learn about running a business. Hence, I decided to pursue my MBA after spending more than 10 years in software industry.

With this new goal in mind, my MBA journey got kickstarted and in summar of 2018, I will be officially start this journey at Olin Business School at Washington University, St. Louis

I hope to utilize the next 2 years to sharpen my business knowledge and continue to experiment with my ideas and bring to life few of them ! Let's see what will happen but I am quite excited to start this new phase in my life.

The dreams of yesterday are the hopes of today and the reality of tomorrow. Science has not yet mastered prophecy. We predict too much for the next year and yet far too little for the next ten.
